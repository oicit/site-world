01

Taste, frozen as tokens

You pick one of three style tiles; the winner becomes theme.css. After that sections may only reference tokens — the QA harness greps for raw hex and fails the build. Drift is an error, not a vibe.

02

Verified sections, not codegen

Pages assemble from a library of self-contained templates — mobile behavior, reduced-motion, CJK text effects already debugged.

03

A screenshot QA loop

Playwright shoots desktop, phone and reduced-motion; a 10-dimension rubric scores the shots. Below 4/5 anywhere and it iterates.

04

A library that compounds

After each project you harvest sections back in. Your tenth site beats your first — one-shot generators start from zero every time.

Fig. 02 — the pipeline, three moves

STEP 01 / BRIEF → TILES

Decide taste once.

The skill interviews you, writes a brief to a file, renders three style tiles as real pages. You pick. The winner freezes into theme.css — every later step is bound to it.

STEP 02 / BLUEPRINT → BUILD

Assemble, don't improvise.

Sections come from a catalog — heroes, scrollytelling, proof bands — each token-only. You cut and reorder the blueprint before a single line is written.

STEP 03 / QA → SHIP

Pass, or iterate.

Screenshots at two widths plus reduced-motion; overflow, console, token and weight checks; a scored rubric. It loops until every dimension clears — then hands you the site and the scorecard.
