Four disciplines separate a process from a prompt.

The core idea

Taste, frozen as tokens

You pick one of three style tiles; the winner becomes theme.css design tokens. From then on, sections may only reference tokens — the QA harness greps for raw hex and fails the build. Style drift becomes a detectable error, not a vibe.

Verified sections

Pages assemble from self-contained templates — mobile behavior, reduced-motion, CJK text effects already debugged.

Screenshot QA loop

Playwright shoots desktop, phone, and reduced-motion; a 10-dimension rubric scores the shots. Below 4/5 anywhere? It iterates.

A library that compounds

After every shipped project you harvest new sections back into the library. Your tenth site is better than your first — one-shot generators start from zero every time.

01

Brief, then three tiles.

The skill interviews you for the page's one job, writes a creative brief to a file, and renders three contrasting style tiles as real pages. You pick. The winner freezes into theme.css.

brief.md → theme.css
02

Blueprint, then assembly.

Sections come from a catalog — heroes, scrollytelling, bento grids, proof bands — each self-contained and token-only. You cut and reorder the blueprint before a line is written.

INDEX.md → index.html
03

QA until it passes.

Full-page screenshots at two widths plus reduced-motion; overflow, console, token, and weight checks; a scored rubric. The loop repeats until every dimension clears — then you get the site and the scorecard.
